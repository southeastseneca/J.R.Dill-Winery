About

J.R. Dill Winery

J.R. Dill Winery sits on the southeast shore of Seneca Lake in Burdett — a small-batch, handcrafted winery about 6 miles north of Watkins Glen that has built a devoted following for its genuinely welcoming atmosphere and quality estate wines. Founded in 2009, J.R. Dill is a hands-on operation: every wine is crafted from 100% Seneca Lake grapes with meticulous attention at every step.

The tasting room is one of the more distinctive on Route 414 — the lake glass bar is a conversation piece, featuring years of beach glass collected from right here at Seneca Lake. Inside, historic photography lines the walls alongside a view of the production area. The covered deck outside offers gorgeous Seneca Lake views and is the spot to settle in with a flight and a snack menu that goes well beyond the usual cheese and crackers.

No reservations required for tastings — walk in any day the winery is open and you'll be welcomed. The snack menu (pretzels with beer cheese, meat and cheese platters, artichoke spinach dip, meatball nachos) makes this a great mid-trail stop for groups who want something to eat alongside their pours. Bubbles and Breakfast events on select summer Sundays are a seasonal highlight not to miss.

The Wines

What's Pouring

All wines are handcrafted from 100% Seneca Lake grapes — dry to sweet, red to white, sparkling and rosé. Flights and by-the-glass available daily.

  • Rosé ★Consistently one of the highlights — bright, elegant, and beautifully made from estate fruit
  • SparklingEffervescent and food-friendly — the Bubbles and Breakfast crowd favorite
  • RieslingDry to semi-sweet expressions — textbook Seneca Lake white wine
  • Cabernet FrancEstate red with Finger Lakes character — light-footed and expressive
  • Whites & BlendsA range of dry and off-dry whites — ask what's current on the tasting menu
  • CocktailsPink Dink, Bunny Lebowski, Cosmo — made with Deep Lake Vodka

Current wine list at jrdillwinery.com — follow @j.r.dill on Instagram for latest updates.

Plan Your Visit

Tips for Visitors

  • Start with the rosé — it's consistently one of the best on the east shore and often the first to sell out.
  • Grab a spot on the covered deck — the shaded Seneca Lake view is outstanding, especially in the afternoon.
  • Order food — the snack menu is genuinely good and makes this a great mid-day stop rather than just a tasting.
  • Bubbles and Breakfast on select Sundays — $25 for a full breakfast buffet and a mimosa. Reserve ahead.
  • Dogs are welcome — bring them along for the walk around the property.
  • 6 miles north of Watkins Glen on Route 414 — easy to pair with a state park visit or a morning at the gorge.
